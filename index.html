🎒 Bags — What Goes Where

Daypack (10–15L) — carry on treks

  • Must have rain cover — monsoon downpours soak packs fast
  • One per person for Matheran, Lohagad & Devkund walks
  • Pack only: water, snacks, rain gear, phone, small first-aid
  • Dry bag or ziplock inside for phone & wallet

Main luggage (40–50L duffel or suitcase)

  • Stays in car or locked at homestay during day treks
  • Use plastic covers or garbage bags inside — car boot gets wet
  • Pack a separate wet-clothes bag for muddy shoes & ponchos

Optional but useful

  • Trekking poles — slippery Lohagad descent & Devkund trail
  • Small waist pouch for Matheran bazaar (no vehicles)

👟 Footwear & Clothing

Footwear

  • Waterproof trekking shoes — deep anti-skid tread, ankle support
  • Break them in before the trip — blisters ruin fort treks
  • Flip-flops or sandals for villa/homestay evenings
  • Extra socks × 3 per person — wet feet are unavoidable

Clothing

  • Quick-dry synthetic tops & track pants × 3 sets — no cotton, no jeans
  • Full-sleeve shirt for Devkund forest trek (branches & leeches)
  • Rain poncho, not umbrella — keeps both hands free on trails
  • Fleece or light jacket (Mahabaleshwar drops to ~15°C)
  • Microfiber towel — dries fast, packs small
  • Cap with brim — rain off your face at waterfall viewpoints

🧰 Gear, Tech & Hydration

  • Reusable water bottle — 1.5–2L per person on trek days
  • ORS / electrolyte sachets × 7 (humidity drains you fast)
  • Headlamp or torch — 6am starts, power cuts in hill towns
  • Power bank (10,000 mAh) — cold + rain kills phone batteries
  • Waterproof phone pouch — non-negotiable in monsoon
  • Whistle — light, cheap, useful if someone separates on a trail
  • Offline Google Maps downloaded for every stop & trek route
  • Snacks: energy bars, dry fruits, peanut chikki for long drives
  • Plastic bags — wet clothes, muddy shoes, trash on treks

💊 Health, Safety & Documents

First aid & monsoon extras

  • Bandages, antiseptic wipes, paracetamol, blister plasters
  • Anti-leech: salt pouch or Odomos — Devkund jungle section
  • Mosquito repellent — forest trails & homestays
  • Personal prescription medicines × full trip supply
  • Hand sanitiser & basic toiletries

Documents & cash

  • ID copies in waterproof pouch (Aadhaar / driving licence)
  • ₹2,000–3,000 cash — ATMs unreliable on ghat roads
  • Car rental agreement & insurance papers
  • Train tickets & homestay booking confirmations (screenshots)

⚠️ Safety Rules

  • Check IMD Maharashtra app before every trek
  • Red/Orange alert = skip exposed ridges & fort summits
  • No night driving on ghat roads — especially Tamhini
  • Lohagad Vinchu Kata ridge only if rock is fully dry
  • Devkund requires a local guide — don't enter jungle alone
  • Bhushi Dam steps are very slippery — stay off edges
  • Don't swim in monsoon waterfalls — currents are deadly
  • Tell someone your ETA before each trek starts

🚗 Car Tips

  • 7-seater SUV or Innova — 7 people + 7 bags won't fit a sedan
  • Photograph every scratch on car pickup — before you drive off
  • Full tank before any ghat section
  • Avoid night drives, especially Tamhini Ghat
  • Keep spare tyre, jack & tow rope accessible
  • Download offline maps — signal drops in ghats
  • Pack a small car kit: tissues, wet wipes, umbrella, umbrella bag

💡 Local Tips

  • Chikki: buy from factory outlets, not roadside stalls
  • Mapro Garden: arrive before 2pm to avoid crowds
  • Matheran: no vehicles past Dasturi Naka — park & walk
  • Strawberry cream at Lodwick Point — worth the stop
  • Mulberry wine at Mahabaleshwar Bazaar
  • Devkund guide fee: ₹300–500 per group — hire at base village
  • Carry change for parking at Lonavala & Mahabaleshwar viewpoints
